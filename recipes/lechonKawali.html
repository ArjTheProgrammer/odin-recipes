Lechon Kawali

Description

Lechon kawali or Pan-roasted pork to some is different from Lechon, the well known Filipino dish which is also the national dish of the Philippines. While lechon is cooked in a pit filled with flamed charcoal, Lechon kawali is cooked by boiling then later deep frying a portion of the pig (pork belly usually) .

Lechon kawali with a cup of rice please! This is my regular order at least once a week when I was in high school. Our school canteen serves one of the best versions of this dish. I like it best when the pork just got out of the pot. It is super crispy and hot.

Recipes

  • 2 lbs. pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons whole peppercorn
  • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 3 cups cooking oil
  • 34 ounces water

Instructions

  1. Pour water into a cooking pot and bring it to a boil.
  2. Add the pork belly and 1 tablespoon of salt. Add peppercorns and bay leaves. Boil for 30 minutes, or until the meat is tender.
  3. Remove the meat from the pot and let it cool down for a few minutes.
  4. Spread 1 tablespoon of salt on the pork belly, distributing it evenly over all sections.
  5. Heat oil in a separate cooking pot for deep frying.
  6. Carefully add each piece of boiled pork belly to the hot oil. Fry until crispy. Turn the meat over to fry the other side until crispy as well. **Note:** Be extra careful of oil splatters. You can use a pot cover to shield yourself, but leave a small opening to prevent steam buildup which can cause more splattering.
  7. Remove the cooked pork belly from the pot and arrange on a wire rack to cool.
  8. Slice the pork belly according to your desired serving sizes.
  9. Serve with lechon sauce. Share and enjoy!
